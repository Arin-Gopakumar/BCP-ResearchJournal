Social Studies Research

The Influence of Infinite Scroll Video Content on Cognitive Time

Dean Ketcham '25

This research demonstrates that consumers underestimate the amount of time that has elapsed while scrolling on social media vs. watching a TV show, and is the first to demonstrate a causal relationship between the infinite scrolling on social media and cognitive time. Importantly, despite the fact that consumers do indeed lose track of time while scrolling on social media, it shows that consumers perceive time to have passed at a normal rate, meaning that they are unaware of any changes in cognitive time (an individual's subjective perception of time).
